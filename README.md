# ✈️ Global Aviation Safety & Risk Intelligence Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

The aviation industry generates vast amounts of operational and safety-related data. This project performs an end-to-end analysis of aviation incidents to identify critical safety risks, operational patterns, and long-term industry trends.

Using Python, data analytics techniques, and business intelligence principles, the project investigates airline performance, aircraft reliability, route risk factors, weather impacts, and aviation safety trends over time.

The goal is to transform raw aviation datasets into actionable insights that support risk assessment and decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

* Identify airlines with the highest incident frequency.
* Analyze fatality and injury trends across the aviation industry.
* Evaluate aircraft manufacturers and aircraft types based on safety performance.
* Investigate weather conditions contributing to severe incidents.
* Determine the most critical flight phases associated with fatalities.
* Analyze route-level risk indicators.
* Monitor long-term aviation safety trends.

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The project utilizes five interconnected datasets:

### 1. Incidents Dataset

Contains detailed aviation incident records including:

* Incident ID
* Date and Year
* Airline
* Aircraft Type
* Manufacturer
* Country and Region
* Weather Conditions
* Phase of Flight
* Fatalities
* Injuries

### 2. Airlines Dataset

Contains airline-level operational information:

* Airline Name
* Fleet Size
* Average Fleet Age
* Safety Tier
* Annual Passenger Traffic

### 3. Aircraft Types Dataset

Contains aircraft specifications and safety indicators:

* Aircraft Type
* Manufacturer
* Engine Information
* Seating Capacity
* Safety Metrics

### 4. Routes Dataset

Contains route-level operational metrics:

* Route Information
* Distance
* Passenger Volume
* Risk Index

### 5. Safety Trends Dataset

Contains historical aviation safety trends:

* Year
* Total Incidents
* Total Fatalities
* Fleet Growth
* Passenger Growth
